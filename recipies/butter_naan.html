Butter Naan

Description

There is nothing quite like a basket of hot, glistening Butter Naan to complete an Indian feast. This iconic leavened flatbread is the ultimate vessel for scooping up thick gravies like Kadhai Paneer or Dal Makhani. While traditional naan is slapped against the clay walls of a scorching tandoor oven, our recipe is perfected for the home stovetop. Using a cast-iron skillet or a regular tawa, you can achieve those signature dark charred spots and a "pillowy" stretch that rivals any high-end restaurant. Brushed with melted salted butter and topped with a hint of garlic or kalonji (nigella seeds), this naan is as much an experience as it is a side dish.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups All-Purpose Flour (Maida)
  • 1 tsp Instant Yeast (or 1/2 tsp Baking Soda + 1/2 tsp Baking Powder)
  • 1 tsp Sugar
  • 1/2 tsp Salt
  • 1/4 cup Thick Yogurt (Curd)
  • 2 tbsp Melted Butter or Oil (for the dough)
  • 1/2 cup Warm Water (adjust as needed for a soft dough)
  • 3 tbsp Salted Butter (extra, for brushing)
  • 1 tsp Nigella Seeds (Kalonji) or Sesame Seeds (optional topping)
  • 1 tbsp Fresh Garlic, minced (optional for Garlic Naan)
  • Fresh Cilantro, chopped (for garnish)

Steps

  1. Combine the flour, yeast, sugar, salt, yogurt, and 2 tbsp of melted butter in a large bowl, then gradually add warm water to knead into a soft, smooth dough.
  2. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest in a warm place for about 1 2 hours (or 30 minutes if using baking soda/powder) until it doubles in size.
  3. Punch the risen dough to release the air, divide it into equal-sized balls, and roll each one out into an oval or teardrop shape about 1/4 inch thick.
  4. Sprinkle nigella seeds, garlic, or cilantro on one side and press them firmly into the dough with a rolling pin.
  5. Flip the naan over and brush the plain side generously with water; this helps it stick to the pan for that authentic tandoor-style char.
  6. Place the naan water-side down on a smoking hot iron tawa (griddle), wait for bubbles to appear, then flip the entire pan over the open flame to char the top.
  7. Carefully remove the naan from the pan using a spatula and immediately brush it with plenty of salted butter while it's still piping hot.
