Kadhai Paneer

Description

Bring the authentic flavors of a North Indian dhaba straight to your kitchen. Our Kadhai Paneer is a vibrant, restaurant-style dish that balances bold heat with the natural sweetness of bell peppers and succulent cubes of fresh paneer. What sets this recipe apart is the Kadhai Masala—a hand-pounded blend of toasted coriander seeds and dried red chilies that creates a coarse, aromatic texture you won't find in store-bought powders. Everything is tossed in a rich, tangy tomato-based gravy and finished in a traditional iron wok (kadhai) for smoky, "charred" edge.

Ingredients

  • 250g Paneer (cubed)
  • 1 large Onion (half chopped, half cut into petals)
  • 1 medium Bell Pepper (cut into squares)
  • 3 medium Tomatoes (pureed)
  • 1 tbsp Ginger-Garlic Paste
  • 2 Green Chilies (slit)
  • 2 tbsp Oil or Ghee
  • ¼ tsp Turmeric Powder
  • 1 tsp Kashmiri Red Chili Powder
  • ½ tsp Garam Masala
  • 1 tsp Kasuri Methi (crushed)
  • 1-2 tbsp Fresh Cream (optional)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Fresh Coriander (for garnish)

Steps

  1. Dry roast the coriander seeds, cumin, dried red chilies, and peppercorns until fragrant, then grind into a coarse Kadhai Masala.
  2. Heat oil or ghee in a pan and sauté the chopped onions, ginger-garlic paste, and green chilies until golden and aromatic.
  3. Add the tomato puree, turmeric, and Kashmiri red chili powder, cooking until the oil begins to separate from the masala.
  4. Stir in 2–3 teaspoons of your fresh Kadhai Masala and salt, allowing the flavors to meld over medium heat.
  5. In a separate pan (or the same one), quickly sauté the onion petals and bell peppers for 2 minutes to maintain their crunch.
  6. Fold the sautéed veggies and paneer cubes into the gravy, adding a splash of water if needed to reach your desired consistency.
  7. Sprinkle with garam masala, crushed kasuri methi, and fresh coriander, then stir in a touch of cream for a rich, restaurant-style finish.
